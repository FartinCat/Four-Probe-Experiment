How to Read the Graph
  • Each black dot is one observation: x = 1/T × 10³, y = ln(ρ).
  • The red dashed line is the best-fit linear regression (least-squares).
  • Slope of the line = Eg / (2k). The value is displayed below the graph.
  • Eg = 2 × k × |slope| = 2 × 8.617×10⁻⁵ × |slopeK| eV
  • For p-type Si the slope should be approximately 6500 K, giving Eg ≈ 1.12 eV.
Inference

As temperature increases, the resistivity of Silicon decreases (negative temperature coefficient), confirming semiconductor behaviour. The linear relationship of ln(ρ) vs 1/T validates the exponential model ρ = ρ₀ exp(Eg/2kT). The slope of the graph gives the band gap energy Eg of Silicon.
