Conclusion

The resistivity of the Silicon (p-type) specimen was measured using the Four-Probe Method over a temperature range of 300 K to 450 K. The resistivity decreased exponentially with increasing temperature, confirming semiconductor behaviour. From the slope of the ln(ρ) vs 1/T graph, the band gap energy of Silicon was determined to be {% if reg and reg.Eg %}{{ reg.Eg }} eV{% else %}—{% endif %}, which is in close agreement with the standard value of 1.12 eV.

Precautions
  1. Ensure the four probes are collinear and equally spaced (s = 0.20 cm) before starting.
  2. Apply minimum pressure to the probes — excessive force will crack the Silicon chip.
  3. Always pass current through outer probes (P1, P4) and measure voltage across inner probes (P2, P3).
  4. Keep the current constant at I = 5 mA throughout; do not adjust between readings.
  5. Allow the temperature to stabilise (~2 minutes) at each step before recording the voltage.
  6. Do not exceed 200°C to prevent permanent damage to the specimen.
  7. Use a regulated DC constant current source — AC supply gives erroneous results.
  8. Ensure good thermal contact between the thermocouple and the specimen holder.
  9. Avoid vibrations or mechanical disturbances during measurements.
  10. Take at least 14–16 readings for a reliable linear regression on the ln(ρ) vs 1/T graph.
Sources of Error
  • Non-uniform probe contact pressure causing variable and uncontrolled contact resistance.
  • Thermal lag between heater and specimen — stabilisation time too short between steps.
  • Slightly unequal probe spacing — calibrate the probe unit with a calibrated scale before the experiment.
  • Electromagnetic interference picked up by long millivoltmeter leads.
  • Oxide layer or surface contamination on the specimen surface at probe points.
  • Current source drift over time — verify I before every reading using the milliammeter.
