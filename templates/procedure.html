Step-by-Step Instructions
1
Sample Preparation

Clean the Silicon specimen surface with acetone or IPA to remove any oxide or contamination. Mount it flat on the insulated sample holder. Ensure the surface is smooth at all four probe contact points.

2
Probe Alignment & Contact

Lower the four collinear, equally-spaced tungsten probes (s = 0.20 cm) gently onto the specimen using the micrometer screw. Apply minimum pressure sufficient for stable electrical contact — excessive force will crack the specimen.

⚠ Probe order (left to right): P1 – P2 – P3 – P4. Verify equal spacing with a magnifier if possible.
3
Circuit Connections

Connect the outer probes P1 & P4 to the Constant Current Source (CCS). Connect the inner probes P2 & P3 to the Digital Millivoltmeter (DMV). Double-check polarity.

📌 Critical: Never connect the current source to the inner probes or the voltmeter to the outer probes.
4
Thermocouple Installation

Insert the type-K thermocouple probe into the thermocouple hole in the sample holder, positioned as close to the specimen as possible. Connect to the digital temperature indicator and verify room temperature is displayed correctly.

5
Room Temperature Reading

Set the current source to I = 5 mA. Record the temperature T₁ (≈ 27°C) and the corresponding voltage V₁ from the millivoltmeter. This is observation number 1.

6
Heating & Data Collection

Switch on the oven and set the temperature controller to increase in steps of approximately 10°C. At each step, wait ~2 minutes for temperature to stabilize, then record T (°C) and V (mV) in the Observation Table.

📌 Keep the current I constant at 5 mA throughout. Do not adjust it between readings.
7
Maximum Temperature

Continue until ~175°C (448 K). Record at least 14–16 readings. More readings improve the accuracy of the ln(ρ) vs 1/T regression.

⚠ Do not exceed 200°C — above this temperature the specimen properties may be permanently altered.
8
Cooling Down

Switch off the heater after the last reading. Allow the system to cool naturally to room temperature before removing the specimen or disconnecting probes.

9
Calculations & Graph

Use the Observation and Calculation pages to compute R = V/I, ρ = R·πW/ln2, and ln(ρ) for each reading. Plot ln(ρ) vs 1/T on the Graph page to determine the slope and hence the band gap energy Eg.
